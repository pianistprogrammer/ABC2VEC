ABC2Vec is a self-supervised deep learning model that learns meaningful vector representations (embeddings) of Irish traditional folk music tunes written in ABC notation, addressing a critical gap in music information retrieval research where folk music—the world's largest body of living musical tradition—has been largely overlooked despite representing humanity's oldest and most widely practiced musical tradition passed down through generations via oral transmission. The core challenge ABC2Vec tackles is that folk music exhibits unique characteristics that distinguish it from classical or popular music: tunes evolve through oral tradition with no canonical score but rather exist as families of melodic variants where each performer, region, and context introduces subtle variations; they use modal scales (Dorian, Mixolydian) beyond simple major/minor tonality; they follow strong structural regularities (AABB form with 16-32 bars); and they're standardized in ABC notation, a compact text-based format that the folk community has converged on. The model architecture begins by taking raw ABC notation (like "X:1 T:The Kesh Jig M:6/8 K:G |:GAG GAB|AGA ABd|...") and performs bar-level patchification, splitting the tune into individual bars (each delimited by "|") to reduce sequence length from ~300 characters to ~24 bar tokens, making Transformer processing efficient—each bar is independently embedded through a character-level encoder with mean pooling to create bar representations, then positional embeddings are added to encode structural information about which section of the tune each bar belongs to. These bar embeddings are fed into a 6-layer Transformer encoder with 8 attention heads and 128-dimensional hidden states, where multi-head self-attention allows the model to capture relationships between bars (like how the A section relates to the B section), and the output bar representations are mean-pooled across the sequence to produce a single 128-dimensional dense embedding vector that represents the entire tune in a semantically meaningful way. The model is trained using two complementary self-supervised objectives on 211,524 Irish tunes from the IrishMAN dataset: (1) Masked Music Modeling (MMM), inspired by BERT's masked language modeling, randomly masks 15% of bars and trains the model to reconstruct them from context using an LSTM decoder, forcing the model to learn local melodic patterns and musical grammar by predicting what bars should appear based on surrounding context; and (2) Transposition Invariance (TI), a contrastive learning objective that creates positive pairs by transposing tunes to different keys (since the same tune in D major and G major should be semantically identical in folk music where key choice depends on instrument range) and maximizes their embedding similarity via InfoNCE loss while pushing apart embeddings of unrelated tunes, enforcing pitch-invariant representations. The dataset preprocessing pipeline involves downloading tunes from The Session and ABCnotation.com, normalizing ABC notation by stripping control codes and parsing headers, extracting metadata like tune type (jig/reel/polka inferred from meter and title keywords), key signature (parsing root note and mode), structural statistics (counting bars and sections), deduplicating exact copies via MD5 hashing of tune bodies, and splitting into 94% train (198,893 tunes), 5% validation (10,469 tunes), and 1% test (2,162 tunes), with the dataset exhibiting strong regularities: median 18 bars per tune with AABB structure, 44.9% reels in 4/4 time, 21.3% jigs in 6/8 time, 80.2% in major mode with 11.3% minor and 8.4% modal (Dorian/Mixolydian), and heavy skew toward G (30.5%), D (26.8%), and A (13.9%) keys due to instrumental constraints of fiddle/flute/whistle. Comprehensive ablation experiments systematically evaluated 26+ model variants across three dimensions: training objectives (where MMM-only achieves 72.1% tune type accuracy providing the strongest single signal with +55.4% gain over random baseline, TI adds +6.2% when combined with MMM for 78.3% accuracy, and adding Section Contrastive Learning provides only marginal +0.8% gain, with statistical testing showing MMM+TI is indistinguishable from the full model at p=0.18, justifying the efficiency choice), architectural choices (where 6 layers achieve optimal depth with diminishing returns beyond, 128 dimensions balance accuracy and memory despite 512-dim being only +1.1% better, mean pooling outperforms max/CLS/attention pooling at 78.3%, 8 attention heads are optimal, and bar patchification with positional embeddings provides critical +9.9% gain over character-level tokenization), and component interactions (showing MMM-TI synergy of +4.1% beyond additive effects because MMM learns local melodic patterns while TI enforces global pitch-invariant structure, addressing complementary aspects). The model achieves strong empirical results: 78.3% tune type classification accuracy (significantly above 16.7% chance), 80.5% mode classification accuracy (above 25% chance), effective variant detection with 0.676 cosine similarity for known melodic variants of the same tune versus 0.253 for unrelated tunes, and linear probing experiments showing that musical properties are encoded in distributed representations across embedding dimensions with minimal overlap between feature subspaces (tune type uses dimensions {12,27,33,34,41} while mode uses {1,9,15,28,45}), though unsupervised clustering yields poor results (negative silhouette scores) indicating embeddings are better suited for supervised retrieval than unsupervised discovery. The computational efficiency analysis demonstrates that the selected 6-layer-128-dim configuration achieves 98.7% of the extra-large model's performance (79.3%) at only 14% of the training cost (18 hours vs 127 hours on NVIDIA A100), making it practical for deployment, with alternative configurations available: a lightweight 4-layer-128-dim version (8.2 hours, 76.8% accuracy, only -1.5% drop) for rapid prototyping, and a large 8-layer-256-dim version (45 hours, 79.1% accuracy) for maximum accuracy when compute budget allows. The project makes four key contributions: (1) the ABC2Vec architecture itself—a 6-layer Transformer with bar-level patchification trained via MMM and TI on 216K Irish folk tunes, (2) systematic evaluation benchmarks for folk music embeddings including tune type/mode classification, variant detection, and linear probing protocols, (3) comprehensive empirical analysis with statistical rigor (bootstrap confidence intervals, p-values, ablation studies) demonstrating 77-80% supervised classification accuracy and effective variant detection, and (4) open-source release of pre-trained models, training code, evaluation datasets, and figure generation scripts to facilitate reproducibility and future research in computational folk musicology. The work addresses critical limitations honestly: transposition invariance is only moderate (0.482 similarity for transposed pairs, indicating the TI objective doesn't fully eliminate pitch dependence), unsupervised clustering fails (suggesting architectural constraints may limit further objective stacking), training is limited to Irish tradition (cross-tradition generalization to Scottish/Appalachian/Scandinavian folk requires evaluation), temporal evolution isn't modeled (missing opportunities for studying tune lineage over centuries), and metadata quality varies in crowdsourced data (tune type labels and variant identification are noisy). Future directions include cross-tradition evaluation to test generalization, temporal modeling incorporating tune dating for studying melodic evolution, multimodal extension aligning ABC embeddings with audio recordings and text descriptions, creating standardized folk similarity benchmarks with ground-truth variant labels, and using pre-trained embeddings for downstream generation/harmonization tasks. The paper's architecture diagram visually illustrates the complete pipeline: ABC notation flows through bar patchification (splitting "|:GAG GAB|AGA ABd|..." into individual bar tokens), character embedding with mean pooling creates bar representations h_1 through h_K, these pass through the 6-layer Transformer encoder with multi-head attention, mean pooling produces the final 128-dimensional tune embedding z, while the right side shows both training objectives—MMM masking random bars and predicting them via LSTM decoder, and TI creating positive pairs through transposition and maximizing similarity via contrastive loss—with the combined objective L = λ_MMM·L_MMM + λ_TI·L_TI trained on 211,524 tunes. This represents the first dedicated representation learning model for folk music that addresses the domain's unique characteristics (oral transmission with variants, modal diversity, rhythmic regularity, standardized ABC notation, and the challenge of similarity/variant detection), validated through rigorous empirical analysis meeting standards for top-tier machine learning venues like ICML, NeurIPS, ICLR, or JMLR, with every design choice systematically justified through ablation experiments and statistical testing, providing actionable recommendations for practitioners while honestly discussing limitations and releasing comprehensive resources to enable future research in this underexplored intersection of music information retrieval, computational musicology, and cultural heritage preservation.
